Your wedding day is one of the most photographed days of your life, and your hair deserves every bit as much attention as your dress. At **Fleur de Lis Salon** in Port Orange, FL, we have helped countless brides feel confident and beautiful walking down the aisle. Here is what we have learned over more than 25 years of bridal styling.

## Start with a Bridal Hair Trial

The most important step you can take is scheduling a trial appointment well before the wedding — ideally two to three months in advance. A trial lets you and your stylist work through the look together without any time pressure. You will see exactly how the style holds, how it photographs, and whether any adjustments are needed before the big day.

Bring inspiration photos to your trial, but also be open to your stylist's suggestions. We look at your face shape, hair texture, and the overall feel of your wedding to recommend styles that will truly suit you — not just look good in a photo.

## Consider Your Veil and Accessories Early

Your updo, half-up style, or loose waves all need to work with your veil and any hair accessories you have chosen. Bring those pieces to your trial appointment so we can position them correctly and make sure everything stays secure throughout the day.

Here in Florida, we also know that heat and humidity are real factors. We will recommend products and techniques to help your style hold up even on a warm Port Orange afternoon or evening.

## Plan Your Color Appointment Strategically

If you are planning to color, highlight, or gloss your hair before the wedding, schedule that service about two to four weeks before the date. This allows time for the color to settle and soften slightly, which photographs beautifully. Avoid dramatic color changes in the week before your wedding — fresh color can sometimes need a day or two to look its very best.

## Allow Enough Time on the Wedding Morning

One of the most common mistakes brides make is underestimating how long hair and makeup will take on the wedding morning. For an updo or an intricate style, plan for at least 60 to 90 minutes for hair alone. If you have bridesmaids or family members also getting their hair done, build a schedule and share it with everyone involved well in advance.

## Do Not Wash Your Hair the Morning Of

This surprises many brides: freshly washed hair can actually be harder to style and hold. Hair that was washed the evening before has just enough natural texture and grip to work with. If your hair tends to feel oily, a light dry shampoo can refresh the roots without making hair too slippery to style.

## Choose Products That Will Last

We use professional-grade products in the salon specifically chosen for their hold, longevity, and finish. Ask your stylist what she used during your trial so you can understand what goes into your look. Hairspray, bobby pins, and the right base of texture spray or mousse all play a role in a style that lasts from ceremony to reception.

## Book Early — Especially for Peak Wedding Season

Wedding season in the Port Orange and Daytona Beach area tends to fill up quickly, particularly in spring and fall. We recommend reaching out to our team as soon as you have a wedding date in mind. Early booking ensures you can get a trial appointment scheduled well in advance and secures your wedding-day slot with the stylist you connect with.
